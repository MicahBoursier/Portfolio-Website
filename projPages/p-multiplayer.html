Untitled Multiplayer Game Project

March 2016| Video Game

Project Summary

I always wanted to experiment with multiplayer in my video games so I tried my hand at it. I made a functioning 3d multiplayer survival game to play with my friends. While its content was limited, and it was very small, it was fairly complex, in terms of other games I had made prior.

I used this game as an opportunity to experiment with concepts I hadn't tried before. It had a simple crafting system, an inventory system with equippable and edible items, a farming system, buildings systems, item sharing, and enemies with simple pathfinding ai. The amount of content was fairly minimal, but it was simply a concept for a survival game system that I hadn't tried before. With multiplayer on, it was fun to play around with friends in. The multiplayer networking was by far the hardest thing to write the code for. There was a lot of tangled relationships between clients, that were hard for me to visualize, but I pulled it off enough for it to work.

I abandoned this project to work on other, more unique video games, because I feel that there are enough survival games in existence already. The experience was not useless, however, because many of the skills I learned and concepts I developed can be used in future games. As an experiment, I it was very useful.
