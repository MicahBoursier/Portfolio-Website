Project Summary

In the summer of 2019, I was accepted into the California State Summer School for the Arts (also known as CSSSA) for animation. CSSSA is an intensive, four-week-long program in which you stay on the CalArts campus, and take daily classes on animation (or whatever other discipline you entered for). During the last 2-3 weeks of your stay, you are tasked with producing an animated short film. I chose to make this short film, titled "Pheromones."

The film had to combine at least two different mediums, so I made the background in watercolor, and animated over it digitally. I wanted to convey the similarity between humans and the creaures we share this planet with, to show that we aren't as superior or advanced as we think we are. I wanted to show that our daily struggles are no more significant in the grand scheme of things than the endles toil of ants in the dirt. I always admired animated films that could convey a concept so clearly and so simply, without dialogue.

Three of the students in the CSSSA animation program are granted a scholarship every year, and this short film earned me one of those scholarships. I earned a Herb Alpert foundation scholarship for young artists.
